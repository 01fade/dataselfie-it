  • Is Data Selfie open source?

    Yes! We believe in an open, free, and democratic internet. Our intention with this project is to be totally transparent. Data Selfie will never use your data for research purposes or for commercial purposes as we don’t store it in a data base. You can check out our code at our GitHub account.

  • How does Data Selfie work?

    When you install the Data Selfie Chrome Extension, it will run in the background as you use Facebook on your desktop. Data Selfie will record your active and passive activity. The extension will record: clicks on likes in your newsfeed, clicks on newsfeed links to external sites, duration spent on different posts and the specifics of those posts (authors, images and text) in your newsfeed, anything you type, and time spent on Facebook overall. The more you use Facebook the more data is recorded. You can look at the Data Selfie dashboard and get a sense of what Facebook could know about your personality. We gather these personality insights by using 2 machine learning APIs: Apply Magic Sauce and IBM Watson.

  • Will you store my data somewhere?

    No. Your data is stored locally on your own computer. We do not store your data. We pass only the necessary data through our servers to the APIs - without your personal information such as name, gender, etc. While we trust these two third-parties, they might store anonymized data or use the results to improve their services.

  • What machine learning API services are you using?

    We use two machine learning API services:

     1. We are using the Apply Magic Sauce API that uses Facebook Like ID’s to predict a users psycho-demographic traits based on their digital footprints. You can learn more in the Apply Magic Sauce API documentation.

    Here is the white paper that is the basis for the API:

     2. We are using IBM Watson. Specifically we are using the Personality Insights, Alchemy API, and Tone Analyzer. We may use the Visual Recognition and Language Translation in further releases.

  • How often will I see updated predictions?

    It was important to us to provide Data Selfie for free to the public. All the costs required to keep the project going are coming out of our pockets. With that in mind, in order to keep costs in check, we have to limit the daily API calls so that as many people as possible can use Data Selfie. We hope people will be nice and courteous to the community so that Data Selfie can be available for all. Updated predictions will depend on your Facebook usage and depend on whether or not there is significant change to your data that makes a new predictions plausible.
    If you really want to play with this more, refer to the next question.

  • What data does the extension track?

    The extension tracks: clicks on likes in your newsfeed, clicks on newsfeed links to external sites, duration spent on different posts and the specifics of those posts (authors, images and text) in your newsfeed, anything you type, and time spent on Facebook overall.

  • What languages are supported?

    To analyze the content in the posts you look at Data Selfie only supports English, German, French, Italian, Portuguese, Russian, Spanish and Swedish. We use IBM's AlchemyLanguage service for that. Predictions based on the text you type, only English, Spanish, Japanese and Arabic are supported. We use IBM's Personality Insights service for that. This might change in the future.

  • What data does the extension send to the API?

    Whatever you type in the Facebook platform and the Like IDs of pages you have interacted with the most will be send to our server and then directly to the API. The extension does not collect personal data from your Facebook profile, nor does it collect any ID. So, since there is no record of it, no personally identifiable information will be sent anywhere.

  • What will your API Services do with my data?

    It is common for them to use anonymized data to improve their models to make better predictions in the future.

    For the IBM machine learning, we have "opted out" so that our requests are not logged and not used for training their models. You can read more here: Controlling Request Logging for Watson Services

  • Do you have any connections to Cambridge Analytica?

    There has been a wide spread discussion about how Cambridge Analytica might have used psychometrics (Big 5 or OCEAN) in their efforts in the Brexit referendum and the US Election 2016 (see articles from e.g. Motherboard and Das Magazin). We have no connection to this company and will never have. The University of Cambridge Psychometrics Centre has released a statement to clarify that they are in no way affiliated with Cambridge Analytica.

  • Are you using Facebook’s Graph API?

    We are not using Facebook’s API. What we do is look at the rendered front-end (the DOM) of the Facebook page in your browser – the HTML elements and their content – and with JavaScript the browser extension can detect when you scroll, when you type and when you click something.

  • What permissions does the extension require?

    The extension will ask you for the following permissions: “Read and change your data on api.dataselfie.it and facebook.com” and “Manage your downloads”. Note: “Manage your downloads” is for backing up your data into one single file on your computer.

  • How do I export my data?

    You can export your data by clicking the Data Selfie eye logo in your Chrome browser and selecting "Export Your Data" from the drop-down menu. A JSON file will automatically download into your "Downloads" folder (on a Mac).

  • How do I import my data?

    You can import your data by clicking the Data Selfie eye logo in your Chrome browser and selecting "Import Your Data" from the drop-down menu. Right now we only support JSON format in the same way the exported JSON is structured. We are looking into how we could support the data you can download directly from Facebook.

  • How do I delete my data?

    You can delete your data by clicking the Data Selfie eye logo in your Chrome browser and selecting "Delete Your Data" from the drop-down menu. Note your data is not saved anyway else on your machine, so it will be gone forever.

  • What browser do I need?

    For a good experience, you need Google Chrome 40 or higher. We have only really tested the extension on a Mac.

  • Will you expand to other browsers, e.g. Firefox?

    We believe in an open and democratic internet and hope to have the bandwidth to expand to more browsers. Firefox is first on our list for expansion. The decision to use Chrome was based on the idea that it is the most popular browser and we could reach many more people on this platform.

  • Will you make a mobile version?

    We recognize that the vast majority of social media usage is done on a mobile device. From the beginning of this project we explored making a mobile version. However, there is no way for us to access Facebook’s native mobile application. (And that is a good thing!) Therefore, the only way to make a mobile version would be to launch our own browser as a native application and implement Data Selfie features. We feel that this approach is not in line with our mission of providing exceptional user experience design. Thus, at this time, we have no intention of making a mobile version.

  • Is Data Selfie free?

    Data Selfie has been designed as a open source, free tool that aims to create awareness in society about the erosion of privacy. From the beginning we intended the tool to be used at no cost.

  • How is Data Selfie funded?

    At this time our funding is courtesy of a grant received by the New York City Media Lab. Additional subsidies to the IBM Watson API have been provided by the IBM Global Entrepreneur Program. Grant funding is accessed through highly competitive processes, and thus we cannot guarantee that we will have continuous funding to maintain Data Selfie. Therefore, we inform the end user that we may eventually need to require donations to continue to maintain, improve and sustain the tool in the long-term. We believe that donations are fair and democratic and in the spirit of the open source community.

  • What is the purpose of Data Selfie?

    The Data Selfie purpose is to create awareness among internet users of the value they generate consciously and unconsciously for online services through the commercial exploitation of their personal information. In particular, Data Selfie provides Facebook users with (1) the kind of data Facebook could be tracking about them and (2) the kind of personality insights Facebook could be generating on them by using machine learning algorithms.

    Data Selfie will not use your data for research purposes or for commercial purposes as we don’t store it in a data base.

  • What information will I receive from the Data Selfie extension?

    Data Selfie will provide the following information:
    An aggregated visual overview of your active and passive activity on facebook.com. This includes clicks on likes in your newsfeed, clicks on newsfeed links to external sites, duration spent on different posts and the specifics of those posts (authors, images and text) in your newsfeed, anything you type, time spent on Facebook overall. This information is stored locally in your browser’s directory on your hard drive.

    Based on this information, various predictions are given, this includes but is not limited to predictions personality (Big 5 model), gender, consumer behavior and consumption content topics. As the capabilities of the APIs we are using change, the kinds of predictions that are provided can change in the future. We will update this section accordingly.
